My Story

As a kid, I always wondered why using a computer felt so complicated—like why did updating the OS take hours? Fast forward to my time at York University studying computer science, I took a UI course and it all clicked. Design was the answer to everything I’d been curious about.
At university, I dove into usability and accessibility research, which showed me how thoughtful design can make technology truly inclusive. After graduating, I worked at PointClickCare, where I learned about agile workflows and how design impacts real people—healthcare workers, in this case. Then at Beanfield, I spent four years crafting internal tools to streamline workflows and make life easier for teams. Along the way, I’ve learned that great design isn’t just about pixels—it’s about people, businesses, and connection.
Now, I’m driven by the idea of improving workflows and solving problems, one problem solved at a time.
I live in Toronto with my chihuahua bestie, Monse. When I’m not designing, you’ll find me sewing, crafting, cooking, or putting together cute outfits. Oh, and I’m a huge fan of psychology and love hearing people’s stories!
