Visualize

Why

Problem definition and use case

Harmony visualize is a direct response to needs of PointClickCare user personas Casey and Patty. Casey and Patty personas both work at the hospital. Casey is responsible for the entire care period planning of senior patients who stay at her hospital. Patty is responsible for keeping up with a Preffred Provider Network (PPN) for her hospital where Casey can discharge patients to.

Who




In senior care, there is the concept of care period; Care period refers to 90 days of care starting from the day a patient is hospitalized.
Payers pay the hospital in bundled payments based on the type of the care patients receive.

  • Make sure patients receive the proper care they need
  • Reduce the time patients spend at the hospital, without risking their well-being
  • Reduce the risk of patients coming back to the hospital, while keeping their stay at the SNF as short as possible


Design process

As-Is scenario

Casey has to follow up with her patients after they leave the hospital. Patients get discharged to either a senior nursing facility, or back to their community. Casey follows up with staff from SNF or patinet's family to make sure care plan is being followed, and patinet is not under rehospitalization risk. i.e patient is getting the proper care, taking their medications, therapy, and right activities.
Before Visualize 2.0, most of this communication happened over the phone. Casey had a hard time tracking down hospital staff to get this information. She often had to drive to senior nursing facilities to follow up and check up on her patients in person, since most of the information she recieved was outdated and inaccurate to the most recent events.

Challenges

  • Information architecture: We knew we wanted to create two different UIs for patty and casey, but we did not know how their workflow will start from a home page (overview page). We decided to land users on different pages depending on their roles. The final product is intuitive to both personas.
  • Visualize 2.0 is the first PointClickCare application that uses Power BI reports. Older insights application used react based data visualization libraries, and we have a design library of interactive components for data visualizations. In the beginning of this project, not only engineering teams needed to resolve complexities of using Power BI reports, but also designers had to find a way to bring consistency and design guidelines to Power BI reports. My team and I created a set of guidelines for PointClickCare Power BI applications, including guidelines on using visualizations, page setup, component details, and more.
  • Some of the UI pages are Power BI reports, and some are react and follow PointClickCare design library. This means we needed to be extra careful with our Power BI design guidelines, and make sure users don't feel any difference between pages that are based on Power BI and react.
  • Covid-19 pandemic made it extremely difficult to perform user testing on later iterations with our personas.
  • The pandemic also introduced an oppurtunity to use Visualize 2.0 to help reduce spread of Covid-19. Designing this new feature in a short amount of time was challenging because it was unknown how much access senior nursing facilities were going to have to tests.
  • Power BI reports are not responsive out of the box, so we had to decide on a certain screen size, and write guidelines on sizing of reports.
